Teaching and research are two strands of my DNA. I really love teaching and its my happy place. I am a happy person who is slightly above average in multiple things

Life was mis oriented in my life till year 2010. That was the year I completed a bachelor's degree in engineering. BUT, I had limited skills, poor confidence and was absolutely clueless about where I am heading. I can tell you, it was terrible to be in that state.

I went around attending job interviews with some of my friends. Nothing happened and decided to pack my bags and spent one year preparing for entrance exam towards a masters degree in engineering. August 8<sup>th</sup> 2010 was my life's turning point at age 22!

That was the day I boarded a train to Hyderabad (Biriyani city or city of pearls). I managed to find a cheap place for accommodation. That was the congested room I ever lived in my life (10 people crammed into a small hall with half a feet corridor). First time in my life I found engineering fascinating!

I had wonderful teachers who knew the topics in and out. Life took a U turn:rewind: when I had to cut short my stay, leaving coaching midway, pack my bags and return home. This phase was tough for me financially, emotionally and in whatever way imaginable.

There was a silver lining when I got admission into a government college for my masters. Those 2 years (2011-2013) were great for me and I spent each day trying to make up for my lost skills. I actively engaged in community teaching and volunteering. Financial status was still tough and I was lucky to have great friends to support me. With the help from one of my batch mate, I started taking coaching classes (banking, clerical jobs, MBA, what not). This helped me to stand on my feet and earn some amount to support myself.

I was placement coordinator for my batch and around second year of my masters degree I started working in a research project in the area of "Brain Computer Interfaces" using EEG technique. Aim of the study was to validate the potential of using BCI in hemiplegic patients. That was the time I first heard about EEGLAB software!

I got a campus placement in one of the respectable MNC with a decent salary package in Garden city (Bengaluru). I worked for one year as an associate software engineering in the domain of SAP. Those were great days and I started active blogging. I came to know about barcamp and participated and later became a speaker twice.

I left my job (didn't like the experience and nature of work) and started preparing for MPhil entrance for Neurosciences course at NIMHANS. That was more  like leap of faith and I managed to get 4th rank (total seats = 4). MPhil was a really good time. I got involved with lot many events happening in and around Bengaluru
